Approaching 3 years in software (wip)

It feels a little longer than 3 years, I'm not sure why. I'm glad it hasn't been that long, I feel like I haven't grown enough, but I assume that's something most people feel and I'm also quite certain that feeling is a trap.

I often find it hard to complain about work, or life in general; I have a privileged job, live in a first world country, have never experienced financial hardship - this list probably goes on for quite a while. On the contrary, I've been finding it hard to be overjoyed about things recently (I know this is sounding quite depressive so far, I promise I'm ok), but it's hard to get excited when you know it doesn't really matter.

You know, what's the point of getting excited about some cool new AI feature that work is implementing when you know AI is now a massive contributor to climate change (something that actually matters). It's hard also, because at times it feels like you are going crazy, you think; does anyone else feel this way? Software is especially strange in this regard - people often adopt it as their entire personality...

It becomes a mark against your name if you're not doing 47 side projects after work and maintaining a personal development list. This is obviously not the case for everyone (probably not the case for most people (most of the time, it's not even the case for me!)), and of course there are people who are extremely passionate, regardless of external pressures. There is also always the option of not participating in this - which is all well and good, but sometimes, just sometimes, you feel a little surrounded by an almost hustle culture like attitude.

I'll try to summarise my problem over the next couple of paragraphs. Because it's not that there are people working hard to become better developers, self improvement and learning is absolutely a good thing - the problem lies with what we are actually doing, the end result, andy sometimes the why.

People grind, and to what end? Did the work contribute to a life saving medical device? Is some of your code part of something that is positively impacting the world, or doing anything that matters? Of course the word 'matters' could be defined in a myriad of ways, but I think you get the point. Are we, in the end, just shoveling more coal into this unstoppable, nonsensical capitalist ship.

You have people, where their goal is become a Senior dev at x or y company (insert FAANG company here), just for the imaginary validation that would come with being hired there... That you would now be a 'good' developer and happiness would finally be attained. Why is this is such a popular goal? Why is not popular to want to be a 'good' person? Or work somewhere that almost undoubtedly does 'good'? It's normalised to work hard, to only the company's benefit, or to climb the imaginary rungs and get hired somewhere with more clout... Hard not feel like it's a trap that way too many people are falling into.

And I know, there's 'amazing' engineering cultures/higher salaries at these places, you want to be/get a part of it - that's cool, I get it. It's just when you start to peel back the layers regarding work culture and why, it's hard to not to see a well disguised, toxic, hustle culture at the core, one that's been fed to us by people who benefit.

This, of course, doesn't apply to everywhere or everyone - but it does it seem to be a rather infectious disease... For example; How many clickbait, rubbish, 'technical' articles are posted to reddit programming subs or HN these days? It's absurd.

To summarise the small rant above - I just wish people cared more about what they were working on/toward. Sometimes it feels like such a race to the bottom.

Anyway, it's not all bad, I'm enjoying the journey I'm on (contrary to everything above) - dev work is fun and rewarding, I've recently dabbled in TDD - which I'm discovering is pretty cool! I'm also learning more about the cloud (this is much less fun). I'd love to do some work in developing new API's or CLI tools; but hey, there's plenty of time for everything...
