Approaching 3 years in software (wip)

It feels a little longer than 3 years, I'm not sure why. I'm glad it hasn't been very long, I feel like I haven't grown enough, but I assume that's something most people feel and I'm also quite certain that feeling is a trap.

I often find it hard to complain about work, or life in general; I have a privileged job, live in a first world country, have never experience financial hardship - this list probably goes on for quite a while. On the contrary, I've been finding it hard to be overjoyed about things recently (I know this is sounding quite depressive so far, I promise I'm ok), but it's hard to get excited when you know it doesn't really matter.

You know, what's the point of getting excited about some cool new AI feature that work is implementing when you know AI is now a massive contributor to climate change (something that actually matters). It's hard also, because at times it feels like you are going crazy, you think; does anyone else feel this way? Software is especially strange in this regard - people often adopt it as their entire personality...

It becomes a mark against your name if you're not doing 47 side projects after work and maintaining a personal development list. This is obviously not the case for everyone (probably not the case for most people), and of course there are people who are extremely passionate, regardless of external pressures. There is also always the option of not participating in this - this is all well and good, but sometimes, just sometimes, you feel a little surrounded by an almost hustle culture like attitude.

I'll try to surmise my problem over the next couple of paragraphs. Because it's not that there are people working hard to become better developers, self improvement and learning is absolutely a good thing - the problem lies with what we are actually doing, the end result.
