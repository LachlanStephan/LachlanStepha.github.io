La La Land

It's 2024, a slightly strange film came out ~8years ago - La La Land.

I think it's a good thing that I'm 8 years late on this one, I'm pretty convinced 18yr old me was an idiot - and I may not have appreciated the spectacle that is La La Land.

The first ~20mins, I wasn't sure how I was going to resonate with the film, I'm very jaded in recent times - simply, I couldn't see myself feeling empathetic toward or loving the characters. Trying to be successful in these areas, we have too many people attempting, I don't think it's something we should be encouraging. Although, with the 2 leads being who they are, it was probably only a matter of time.

About 5mins later, I'm more invested in these 2 people than I am myself, I'm completely sucked in.

The dance sequences were gorgeous and fun - there's the iconic one atop the hill (you know the one I mean), I could watch re-watch this over and over... It makes me want to take dance lessons.

I think we all like to get lost in a few things; hope, love, dreams... Wholly shit, this was that and so much more.

This film made me feel like a hopeful kid again, and for that, I'm grateful - It was utterly enchanting and I believe this will sit me with for quite some time - it was more emotional than I could've anticipated.

I can't stop thinking about her day dream, her what if...
